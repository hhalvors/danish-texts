Danish Philosophical Texts

This page collects transcriptions and English translations of Danish philosophical works, primarily from the nineteenth century. All source texts are in the public domain; scans are drawn from the Royal Danish Library (Det Kongelige Bibliotek). The LaTeX sources for all documents are on GitHub.

Translations are works in progress. Corrections and suggestions are welcome.

Rasmus Nielsen (1809–1884)

Nielsen held the chair of philosophy at the University of Copenhagen from 1841 until his death. He began as a Hegelian but came under Kierkegaard's influence in the 1840s, developing a distinctive position on the absolute heterogeneity of faith and knowledge that made him one of the most discussed—and contested—philosophers in Denmark during the second half of the century. His work is a central point of reference for understanding both the Kierkegaard reception and the Danish encounter with Darwinism and scientific naturalism.

Religionsphilosophie (Copenhagen: Gyldendal, 1869)

Nielsen's most systematic work. Argues that faith and knowledge are as principles absolutely heterogeneous, and develops a philosophy of religion that it calls an "inverted science" — one that takes the religious principle as primary rather than deriving religion from speculative reason (Hegel) or dissolving it through critical reduction (Feuerbach). "Et Synspunkt for Darwinismen" (1873)

Published in For Idé og Virkelighed, 1873, pp. 449–459. Nielsen defends Darwin against philosophical objections, arguing that Darwinism is a matter of empirical method rather than natural philosophy, and that the religious concept of creation is a concept of faith, not of knowledge.

Harald Høffding (1843–1931)

Høffding was Nielsen's student and eventual successor as the dominant figure in Danish philosophy. He moved toward a broadly empiricist and psychologistic position, engaging systematically with Darwin, Spencer, and the emerging sciences of mind. His Psykologi i Omrids (1882) and Den nyere Filosofis Historie (1894–1895) were internationally influential. His relationship with Niels Bohr has attracted recent scholarly attention.

"Filosofien og Darwinismen" (1874)

Published in For Idé og Virkelighed, 1874. Subjects Darwinism to epistemological scrutiny while arguing that its significance for Verdensanskuelse (worldview) is separable from its empirical confirmation.

On realism in science and faith; engages with the relationship between scientific knowledge and religious belief in the wake of Darwinism.
