## What this piece is
Brudstykke IV of Efterladte Skrifter vol. 3 — Møller's materials toward a
never-finished treatise on **affectation** (inauthenticity / self-deception),
one of his signature ethical-psychological concepts. Two parts (per the editor's
title-note):
1. **Connected treatment** (begun 1837), pp. 163–173: affectation defined as a
   compound of *Falskhed* (falsity) and *Selvbedrag* (self-deception) directed
   at an aim outside the action; a scale of personal truth (natural desire →
   ancient virtue → rational autonomy → religiously hallowed will); the three
   degrees — **momentary**, **fixed**, and **changing** affectation (labels
   letterspaced in the print, kept as `\emph{}`).
2. **Editor's thematic grouping** of the author's scattered aphoristic notes,
   pp. 173–188, separated by centred rules (`\ombreak`): affectation of feeling,
   of art-enthusiasm, of opposition, of style/pedantry, of misanthropy, etc. —
   telegraphic, epigrammatic, rich in cultural diagnosis. Ends p. 188 with a
   closing double-rule.

Notable for the Bohr-epistemology through-line: the **"two sides" passage** on
p. 187 — "Da enhver Sag kan sees fra to Sider, var det tilfældigt, fra hvilken
Side den øieblikkeligen blev seet" — with the memorable **China frog-bundle**
image, and the recurring theme that the same reality is seen differently
according to standpoint. Directly pairs with **§ 29** of the Moralphilosophi
lectures (also just transcribed).
